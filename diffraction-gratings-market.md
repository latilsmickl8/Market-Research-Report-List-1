<p><p>The diffraction gratings market is primarily segmented into ruled gratings and holographic gratings. Ruled gratings consist of parallel grooves etched onto a substrate, enhancing light dispersion and often used in spectrometers. Holographic gratings, on the other hand, are created using laser interference patterns, allowing for better efficiency and resolution, making them ideal for advanced applications in telecommunications and spectroscopy. Both types cater to diverse industries, including scientific research, telecommunications, and optical instrumentation, driving market growth.</p></p>
