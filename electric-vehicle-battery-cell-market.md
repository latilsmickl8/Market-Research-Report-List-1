<p><p>The electric vehicle battery cell market comprises several key types: </p><p>1. **NCM/NCA** (Nickel Cobalt Manganese/Nickel Cobalt Aluminum) cells offer high energy density, making them popular for long-range EVs.  </p><p>2. **LFP** (Lithium Iron Phosphate) cells prioritize safety and longevity, ideal for budget-friendly models.  </p><p>3. **LCO** (Lithium Cobalt Oxide) cells are known for high energy density but have limited thermal stability.  </p><p>4. **LMO** (Lithium Manganese Oxide) cells provide good thermal stability and are often used in power tools and EVs.  </p><p>5. **Others** encompass emerging chemistries improving performance and sustainability.</p></p>
