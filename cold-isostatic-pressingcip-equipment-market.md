<p><p>Cold Isostatic Pressing (CIP) equipment is categorized into two primary types: Dry Bag Pressing and Wet Bag Pressing. Dry Bag Pressing uses a flexible rubber bag without any fluid, applying pressure uniformly to the powder material. In contrast, Wet Bag Pressing involves a rubber bag filled with hydraulic fluid, allowing for better compaction and enhanced density in the final product. Both methods are crucial in manufacturing ceramics, metals, and composite materials, providing high precision and improved physical properties.</p></p>
