<p><p>Ablation technology encompasses various methods for tissue removal or destruction, including electrical, ultrasound, radiation, and radiofrequency. Electrical ablation uses heat generated from electrical current, often in cardiac procedures. Ultrasound ablation employs high-frequency sound waves to disrupt tissue, commonly used in soft tissue applications. Radiation ablation utilizes ionizing radiation to target and destroy abnormal cells, prevalent in cancer treatments. Radiofrequency ablation delivers alternating current to produce heat, effectively treating conditions like tumors and cardiac arrhythmias by selectively destroying tissue.</p></p>
