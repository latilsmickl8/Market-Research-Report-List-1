<p><p>The orthopedic trauma fixation product market comprises various types, including internal fixation and external fixation. Internal fixation involves plates, screws, nails, and other devices used to stabilize fractured bones within the body. Plates and screws provide strong support, while nails are inserted into the medullary canal for long bone fractures. Other internal fixation methods encompass diverse devices tailored for specific needs. External fixation involves the use of frames and pins outside the body to stabilize fractures, offering adjustable support for complex injuries.</p></p>
