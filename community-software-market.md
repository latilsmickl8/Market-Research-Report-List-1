<p><p>Community software can be categorized into two main market types: on-premise and cloud-based. On-premise solutions are installed and run on a user's local hardware, offering greater control and customization but requiring significant IT resources for maintenance and updates. In contrast, cloud-based community software is hosted on remote servers, allowing easier access and collaboration without heavy infrastructure investments. This model typically offers scalability and automatic updates, making it attractive for organizations seeking flexibility and cost-effectiveness.</p></p>
