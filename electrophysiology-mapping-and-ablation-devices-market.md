Conventional devices include standard mapping catheters and radiofrequency ablation systems. Advanced systems utilize enhanced imaging and mapping techniques for improved precision. Ultrasound ablation employs sound waves to destroy abnormal tissue, while cryoablation uses extreme cold for similar purposes. Microwave ablation harnesses microwave energy for targeted tissue destruction. These diverse technologies cater to different clinical needs, enhancing treatment efficacy and patient outcomes.</p></p>
